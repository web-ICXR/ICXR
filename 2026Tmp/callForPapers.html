Call for Papers

The International Conference on Extended Reality (ICXR) is the premier academic forum dedicated to advancing research and innovation in Virtual Reality (VR), Augmented Reality (AR), Mixed Reality (MR) and related technologies. Organized by the China Computer Federation (CCF) and hosted by its Virtual Reality and Visualization Technology Committee, ICXR 2026 continues its mission as part of the CCF's High-Quality International Academic Conference Incubation Program. The conference has been selected for inclusion in the CCF Recommended List of International Conferences.

ICXR 2026 seeks original, high-quality papers in all areas related to extended reality (XR) technologies. We particularly encourage innovative research that explores the intersection of XR with Artificial Intelligence (AI), including but not limited to AI-driven content generation, intelligent interaction systems, machine learning for XR applications, and neural rendering techniques.

The conference will feature a rigorous peer-review process with two review cycles. Authors of papers that receive positive initial reviews will have the opportunity to submit revised versions addressing reviewers' comments.

Date & Venue

The conference will be held on November 12–13, 2026, in Guangzhou, a dynamic global innovation hub at the heart of the Greater Bay Area (GBA). Delegates will benefit from world-class facilities, vibrant industry-academia networking opportunities, and proximity to one of the world’s most active tech ecosystems.

Join leading researchers, industry pioneers, and visionary developers to exchange ideas, forge collaborations, and accelerate the future of XR.

Submission Guidelines

Paper Categories

Each research paper should provide a contribution covering one or more of the following categories:

  • Methodological papers: Advances in theories and methods of XR, such as ethical issues, theories on presence, or human factors.
  • Technical papers: Advancements in algorithms or devices critical to XR development such as input, display, user interaction, or tracking.
  • Application papers: Novel applications of XR technologies to solve interesting problems in various domains, including evaluations of success in the given application area.
  • Systems papers: Integration of techniques and technologies to produce effective XR systems, conveying lessons learned in the process.

Format Requirements

  • Papers should be submitted in PDF format.
  • The normal length is 12-15 pages of main content (excluding references and appendices).
  • References should not exceed 3 pages.
  • Papers must be formatted using the Springer LNCS conference template (LaTeX Template, Word Template).
  • All submissions must be in English.

Review Process

ICXR 2026 employs a double-blind review process. Authors must ensure that their names and affiliations do not appear in the manuscript. Failure to anonymize submissions will result in desk rejection.

The review process consists of two cycles. The first cycle determines if a submission is eligible for conditional acceptance with a minor revision. The second cycle consists of a short re-review of the revisions by the coordinator and leads to a final recommendation.
