CCF ICXR 2026 Reviewing Guidelines

These guidelines apply to all individuals involved in the ICXR 2026 review process, including Coordinators (1AC), Program Committee Members (2AC), external reviewers, and Program Chairs.

1. Ethical Standards and Confidentiality

1.1 Fundamental Principles

  • Confidentiality: All manuscripts, reviews, and discussions are strictly confidential. Do not share papers or reviews with colleagues, students, or others.
  • Conflict of Interest (COI): Declare and recuse yourself if you have collaborative, competitive, or personal relationships with authors that may bias your review

1.2 AI Usage Prohibition (Strict)

Using AI to write or assist in writing peer reviews is strictly prohibited. All reviews must be written entirely by the human reviewer without assistance from artificial intelligence systems (including but not limited to ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or other LLMs).

  • Uploading papers to AI systems (Claude AI, Wordtune, ChatGPT, etc.) for analysis or review assistance is strictly forbidden
  • Relying on AI for assessment or scoring is strictly forbidden

Violations of this policy will be considered serious academic misconduct and may result in removal from the review process and future exclusion from ICXR activities. All reviews are expected to reflect the expert human judgment of the reviewer.

1.3 Anonymity Protection

  • Do not reveal submission information to third parties
  • Do not discuss specific submissions on social media or public forums
  • Do not confirm author identities identified through self-citations or writing style

2.2 Role Responsibilities

  • Coordinator (1AC): Sees author identities, recruits 1 external reviewer, leads discussion among reviewers, synthesizes feedback into meta-review, prepares initial recommendation (regular/short/reject), checks revisions by September 20 and makes final recommendations, communicates with authors
  • Program Committee Member (2AC): Blinded to author identities, recruits 1 external reviewer, writes their own substantive review (8-point scale), participates in discussion
  • External Reviewers (2 per paper; recruited by 1AC and 2AC): Provide detailed assessments, blinded to author identities
  • Program Chairs: Review 1AC's final recommendations by September 20, make final Accept or Reject decisions by September 23 (no conditional accept), select high-quality regular papers for journal recommendations

3. Cycle 1: Initial Assessment

3.1 Desk Rejection Criteria

1AC and 2AC may recommend immediate rejection to Program Chairs for:

  • Clear scope violations (outside XR/VR/AR/MR domains)
  • Severe policy violations (non-anonymized submissions, exceeding 15 pages for main content, incorrect formatting)
  • Quality substantially below conference standards (final decision authority: Program Chairs)

3.2 Assessment Scale (8-Point)

All reviewers (2AC and both External Reviewers) evaluate submissions using the following 8-point scale:

Important: This score guides the conditional acceptance decision. Scores 6-8 typically indicate Regular Paper potential, Score 5 indicates either Regular or Short Paper potential, Score 4 indicates Short Paper potential or Rejection, and Scores 1-3 indicate Rejection. 1AC synthesizes all three reviews (2AC + 2 externals) into a meta-review and initial recommendation.

3.3 Regular Paper vs. Short Paper Recommendation

During Cycle 1, 1AC and reviewers should assess whether the submission merits regular paper acceptance or would be more suitable as a short paper. Note: There is no separate short paper submission track; all papers are submitted as full 12-15 page papers.

  • Regular Paper (12-15 pages, oral presentation): Substantial contribution justifying full-length presentation; typically corresponds to scores 6-8; accepted regular papers are presented orally and may be considered for journal recommendations
  • Short Paper (8 pages main content, poster presentation): Interesting and valid contribution that would benefit from condensed presentation; typically corresponds to scores 4-5; suitable for focused technical contributions, preliminary but promising results, or specialized applications; short papers are presented as posters only and are not considered for journal recommendations

3.4 1AC Meta-Review Requirements

1AC must synthesize the three reviews (2AC + 2 externals) into a comprehensive meta-review that includes:

  • Summary of the paper's contribution and its significance to XR
  • Assessment of agreement/disagreement among reviewers
  • Resolution of conflicting opinions
  • Clear justification for the initial recommendation (Regular/Short/Reject)
  • Specific guidance for authors if revision is recommended

3.5 High-Quality Review Requirements (for 2AC and Externals)

Content Standards (Minimum 500 words/~1 page)

Reviews must include:

  • Contribution Summary (1–2 paragraphs): Accurate description and contextualization of significance
  • Detailed Assessment (2–3 paragraphs): Strengths, weaknesses (phrased constructively), methodological adequacy, avoidance of hindsight bias
  • Actionable Recommendations (1 paragraph): Specific suggestions distinguishing "must address" vs "consider if possible"
  • Minor Issues (optional): Typos, grammar, formatting

Prohibited Practices

  • AI-generated reviews: Using AI to write or assist in writing any part of the review
  • Brief/vague reviews without explanation
  • Citation manipulation to influence bibliometrics
  • Ad hominem attacks

3.6 Specific Assessment Criteria

  • Participant Diversity: Verify demographic reporting (gender, age), assess representativeness
  • Technical Rigor: Check algorithm completeness, experimental design, data support for claims

4. Discussion Period (Cycle 1)

  • 1AC leads discussion among 2AC and external reviewers via the review system
  • All reviewers participate in consensus-building; revise assessments based on dialogue
  • 2AC and externals read peer reviews and engage constructively
  • 1AC synthesizes final feedback and prepares meta-review and initial recommendation

5. Cycle 2: Final Decision by Program Chairs

5.1 Revision Evaluation (1AC - By September 20, 2026)

  • 1AC reviews revised manuscripts submitted by September 15
  • Verify authors addressed Cycle 1 comments adequately
  • Check revision scope: Authors may add experiments explicitly requested by reviewers but not new theorems/algorithms beyond original scope
  • Prepare final recommendation report for Program Chairs by September 20, 2026

5.2 Final Decision Authority (Program Chairs - September 23, 2026)

Program Chairs review:

  • 1AC's final recommendation (submitted September 20)
  • Revised manuscript
  • All Cycle 1 and Cycle 2 materials
  • Author response to comments

Final Decision by September 23, 2026 (Binary - No Conditional Accept, No Shepherd):

  • Accept (Regular): Full oral presentation; scores 6-8 may receive journal recommendation
  • Accept (Short): 8 pages, poster only; scores 4-5; not eligible for journal recommendations
  • Reject: Typically scores 1-3 or failed revision

Note: Program Chairs make definitive Accept or Reject decisions by September 23, specifying whether accepted papers are Regular (oral) or Short (poster). Journal recommendations are determined by Program Chairs based on quality and scope alignment; authors cannot select journals.

6. Quality Assurance

  • Specific, constructive feedback required; inadequate reviews must be supplemented
  • Submit by deadlines; avoid last-minute reviews
